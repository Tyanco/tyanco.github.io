About Me

Hello and welcome!

I am a climate and sustainability risk researcher with over six years of experience spanning financial markets, environmental science, disaster risk, renewable energy, and climate policy. I am currently pursuing a PhD in Earth & Climate Sciences at Duke University, with Dr. Martin Doyle and Dr. Brian McAdoo. My research examines how interactions between policy decisions, climate change, natural disasters, and ecosystem degradation strains financial systems and ultimately affect communities, livelihoods, and long-term resilience. My work also focuses on developing market-based, nature-positive solutions for climate adaptation, leveraging financial mechanisms such as catastrophe bonds, parametric insurance, and novel forms of resilience financing

Before starting my PhD I spent 5 years in the Insurance Industry working at the intersection of climate science & policy, catastrophe risk, and insurance/investment markets on Liberty Mutual‚Äôs Catastrophe & Climate Analytics Research & Development Team. I have also spent time working in coastal ecological research and restoration and renewable energy policy.
